    gargammel: an ancient DNA simulator

    About

    gargammel is a pipeline to simulate ancient DNA (aDNA) from a set of known references. gargammel seeks to emulate the in vivo process that leads to the sequencing of aDNA fragments. First fragments are collected from a set of reference sequences. these reference sequences are designed to represent the endogenous DNA, the contaminant from the same species (e.g. present-day humans) and bacterial sequences The patterns of mis-incorporations of ancient DNA fragments can be also accounted for. Second, aDNA damage is added to those fragments. Sequencing adapters are appended to form reads Of a specific length and, finally, sequencing errors with corresponding quality scores are applied to the reads. The resulting data is a set of Illumina reads that can be used to ascertain certain hypotheses about aDNA. Here is a potential set of questions that could be answered using our pipeline:

    1. Impact of present-day human contamination on various statistics used in population genetics
    2. Influence of high levels of the deamination on mapping
    3. Impact of a high number of bacterial sequences on alignment to a specific reference
    To simulate Illumina sequencing errors, we use the ART package.
